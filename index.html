Lamborghini Timeline

  • 1964 Lamborghini 350 GT (1964): The first production car by Lamborghini, featuring a 3.5-liter V12 engine and timeless design.
  • 1966 Lamborghini Miura (1966): Iconic mid-engine supercar, known for its revolutionary design and high-performance capabilities.
  • 1968 Lamborghini Espada (1968): A grand tourer with four seats, combining speed with spacious luxury for long-distance drives. Lamborghini Islero (1968): Elegant 2+2 coupe, powered by a V12 engine, offering a balance of performance and comfort.
  • 1970 Lamborghini Jarama (1970): A front-engined grand tourer with unique styling, offering a blend of performance and practicality.
  • 1973 Lamborghini Urraco (1973): Compact sports car with a V8 engine, emphasizing a more affordable entry into the Lamborghini world.
  • 1974 Lamborghini Countach (1974): Legendary wedge-shaped supercar, setting new standards in design and speed during its era.
  • 1976 Lamborghini Silhouette (1976): Mid-engine Targa-top sports car, known for its distinctive design and exhilarating performance.
  • 1981 Lamborghini Jalpa (1981): A successor to the Urraco, featuring a V8 engine and a more refined design.
  • 1986 Lamborghini LM002 (1986): The "Rambo Lambo," an off-road luxury SUV, powered by a V12 engine, built for extreme terrains.
  • 1990 Lamborghini Diablo (1990): Flagship supercar with futuristic design, boasting impressive speed and power.
  • 2001 Lamborghini Murciélago (2001): All-wheel-drive flagship, featuring a powerful V12 engine and iconic scissor doors.
  • 2003 Lamborghini Gallardo (2003): Entry-level supercar, known for its agility and V10 engine, making Lamborghini ownership more accessible.
  • 2008 Lamborghini Reventón (2008): Limited edition hypercar, inspired by fighter jets, with a design that pushes the boundaries of aerodynamics.
  • 2011 Lamborghini Aventador (2011): V12-powered flagship supercar, featuring cutting-edge technology and aggressive styling.
  • 2014 Lamborghini Huracán (2014): Mid-engine supercar, blending performance and daily usability with a sleek and modern design.
  • 2016 Lamborghini Centenario (2016): Limited edition hypercar celebrating the centenary of the company, combining extreme performance with exclusivity.
  • 2018 Lamborghini Urus (2018): High-performance SUV, combining the practicality of an SUV with Lamborghini's speed and luxury
  • 2019 Lamborghini Sián (2019): Hybrid hypercar, featuring a V12 engine paired with a supercapacitor system for unprecedented performance.
